## 15.6.2 Hierarchical persistence

Composite formation naturally produces hierarchical structures.
Let a persistent object be denoted
$$
O_1.
$$
Pair formation produces
$$
O_2 = O_1 + O_1.
$$
Three-body composites produce
$$
O_3 = O_2 + O_1.
$$
More generally
$$
O_N = \sum_{i=1}^{N} O_1.
$$
Persistence properties then depend on the collective locking energy.

As composite size increases, locking interactions multiply.

$$
E_{lock}^{(N)} \sim N^2 E_0.

$$
E_{form}^{(N)} \sim N E_0.
$$
Thus the locking ratio becomes
$$
\Lambda_{lock}^{(N)} \sim N.
$$
Larger composites therefore possess increasing structural stability.

For a network of (N) nodes the total interaction energy becomes
$$
E_{network}
\sum_{i<j} V_{ij}.

$$
Thus structural locking grows rapidly with system size.
